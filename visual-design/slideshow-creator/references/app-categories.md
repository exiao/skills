# App Category Templates

## Home / Interior Design

**Slide concept:** Same room, same angle, different interior styles.

**Base prompt template (adapt wording for your image gen provider):**
```
Realistic photo of a [STYLE] [room type] in a small flat. [Room dimensions].
Shot from [position]. [Window details]. [Door details]. [Key furniture with exact
positions]. [Floor type]. [Ceiling details]. Natural lighting, phone camera quality.
Portrait orientation.
```

**"Before" rules:**
- Modern but tired, NOT ancient
- Include flat screen TV on wall
- Everyday items: mugs, remote, magazines
- Magnolia walls, basic curtains, no decor

**Style transforms:** Mid-century modern, Scandinavian, Industrial luxe, Coastal, Japandi, Maximalist bohemian

## Beauty / Cosmetics

**Slide concept:** Same face, same angle, progressive enhancement.

**Base prompt template:**
```
Close-up portrait photo of a young woman, [age], [ethnicity], [features],
[hair], [makeup level]. [Expression], looking directly at camera. Natural 
indoor lighting from window to the left. Plain wall background. Phone selfie 
quality, natural skin texture with visible pores. Portrait orientation.
```

**Preservation rules:**
- Face shape PIXEL-PERFECT identical
- Nose, eyes EXACTLY the same
- ALL skin texture preserved (pores, freckles)
- NO skin smoothing or beauty filters
- ONLY change the target feature (lips, lashes, etc.)

---

## Fitness / Body

**Slide concept:** Same person, same pose, progressive transformation.

**Base prompt:** Mirror selfie or gym photo, consistent lighting and background.

**Transform progression:** Current → 1 month → 3 months → 6 months (subtle, believable changes)

---

## Productivity / SaaS

**Slide concept:** Before/after workflow visualization OR results demonstration.

**Approach:** Can use screenshots with text overlays rather than AI images. Show the messy "before" (spreadsheets, chaos) vs clean "after" (organized, automated).

---

## Food / Recipe

**Slide concept:** Same dish, different presentations or same ingredients, different meals.

**Base prompt:** Overhead shot of food on table, consistent tableware and background.

## Finance / Investing (Cartoon Story)

**Slide concept:** Character overcomes financial insecurity or ignorance with the help of the app. 8-slide emotional narrative using illustrated/cartoon style.

**Character archetypes:**
- Young professional who feels behind their peers financially
- Person who lost money and is scared to try again
- Someone whose partner/family dismisses their ability to invest
- College student with $100 who wants to start but feels locked out

**Nano Banana Pro prompt template:**
```
Cartoon illustration of a [age] [gender] [ethnicity] character, [hair], 
[clothing]. [Scene: at desk looking at phone / sitting alone at dinner / 
talking to dismissive partner / celebrating at laptop]. [Expression: 
worried/defeated/surprised/confident]. Warm cinematic lighting, illustrated 
art style, semi-realistic cartoon rendering. Portrait orientation.
```

**8-slide story arc example (Bloom):**

| Slide | Scene | Text overlay |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Character at dinner party, everyone discussing stocks | "My friends talk about\ntheir portfolios and\nI just nod along" |
| 2 | Friend making dismissive comment | "You don't invest?\nWhat do you even do\nwith your money?" |
| 3 | Character alone, scrolling confusing finance content | "Every article assumes\nyou already know\nwhat a P/E ratio is" |
| 4 | Character at laptop, head in hands, red charts | "I bought one stock\nand lost 40% in\ntwo weeks" |
| 5 | Character discovers Bloom on phone, curious expression | "Then my coworker\nshowed me this app\nthat actually explains things" |
| 6 | Character reading an earnings breakdown, lightbulb moment | "Wait... I actually\nunderstand what\nI'm buying now?" |
| 7 | Character confidently reviewing portfolio, green gains | "3 months later and\nI check my portfolio\nwithout panicking" |
| 8 | Back at dinner party, character contributing to conversation | "Now when they talk\nstocks I don't\njust nod along" |

**Hook formulas (finance-specific):**
- "My [person] said I'm terrible with money. That I'd never figure out investing."
- "Everyone my age has a portfolio. I have anxiety."
- "The financial advisor said I needed $50K minimum. I had $200."
- "I lost $3,000 on my first trade and swore I'd never invest again."
- "My partner handles all the money because they think I can't."
