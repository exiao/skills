# Daniel / Cal AI / Sway Growth Playbook

Source: @JosephKChoi interview with Daniel from Cal AI/Sway, May 2026.

## Distribution Before Product

Sway AI built demand before the app existed:
1. Posted actionable dating profile tips on Instagram/TikTok.
2. Added preorder link in bio.
3. Asked followers to DM screenshots for a $15 manual profile review.
4. Used payment, not likes or waitlist emails, as proof of demand.
5. Built the app around the manual workflow people already paid for.

Bloom adaptation: publish AI stock teardown cards or videos, then offer a paid/manual portfolio or watchlist review before automating the workflow.

## Slideshow Batch System

Sway found viral slideshow text from adjacent accounts, adapted it to dating-profile advice, designed reusable Figma/Canva backgrounds, then auto-populated slides from a spreadsheet. This let them batch about a month of content in 1-2 hours.

Use when the format has repeatable variables: ticker, company, guru, mistake, red flag, prompt, score, before/after.

## Three-Screen App Demo

Cal AI creator videos repeatedly show three core screens: camera, loading, food detail page. The product team optimized those screens for instant comprehension because influencer marketing was the primary growth loop.

App idea/product design test:
1. What is the input screen?
2. What is the magical processing or suspense screen?
3. What is the result screen worth showing?

Bloom adaptation: stock/ticker input or portfolio screenshot → AI analysis loading → clear insight card/risk score/actionable watchlist result.

## Content-Niche App Filter

A consumer app idea is stronger when it can plug into an existing viral content niche, not just an App Store category.

Score ideas on:
- Social clarity: can someone understand the app in 3 screens or 5 seconds?
- Novel demo: is the core action visually new enough to feel worth sharing?
- Big market: does the underlying behavior support durable demand?
- Influencer supply: are there many creators, and are they reasonably priced?
- Creator fit: does the app complement the creator's business, or cannibalize it?

Cal AI fit fitness coaches because calorie tracking complements coaching. Sway conflicted with dating coaches because AI profile reviews compete with their paid offer.

## Influencer and Paid Scaling

Sway's organic slideshow/content got to roughly $30-40K MRR. Paid ads using a trusted dating influencer scaled much further, toward about $2M ARR. Lessons:
- Influencers provide guaranteed views; owned UGC depends on virality.
- One creator becomes a creative bottleneck quickly.
- Mild controversy or debate can help if policy-safe because comments are engagement.
- Boosted organic is not automatically good paid creative. Commission explicit ad concepts, then repurpose winners.
