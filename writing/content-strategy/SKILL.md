# Content Strategy

## Purpose

Turn zeitgeist signals into content that connects — then delegate creation, distribution, and optimization to the right tools.

**The flow:** Zeitgeist → Angles & Hooks → Create (via other skills) → Distribute → Analyze

**How to use this skill:** Synthesize, don't recite. When answering a question, pick the 2-3 frameworks most relevant to the specific situation and weave them into a coherent strategy. Don't list every framework in the skill. A strategy that references 12 frameworks is an index, not advice. The user should walk away with "here's what to do in what order" — not "here are all the concepts that exist."

**Every response must include a research step.** Even format-focused or optimization questions need 2-3 sentences on what to research first and which tools to use. Content strategy without audience research is guessing. If the question is narrow (e.g., "plan a repeatable format"), the research step can be short ("Use `grok-search` to find 5 accounts running similar formats; note which variables drive the most views"), but it must exist.

**NOT this skill:**
- Writing tweets or posts → `writer`
- Generating images → `nano-banana-pro`
- Writing articles → `writer`
- Scheduling → `typefully`

## Step 1: Zeitgeist Research

Mine what's working **right now** before writing a single word.

**Sources:**

| Source | What to look for |
|--------|-----------------|
| Reddit/Facebook groups | Exact audience language, recurring complaints, questions |
| Competitor posts (30+ days live) | Proven angles, format patterns, hook structures |
| X/TikTok trending content | Viral formats, cultural hooks, emerging memes |
| Google trends and news | Breaking topics, search-driven angles, seasonal signals |
| Testimonials and reviews | Transformation language, before/after framing |
| Support tickets and FAQs | Objections, misconceptions, friction points |
| TikTok accounts with <1k followers | Viral signals before algo amplification; comment sentiment on what's actually resonating |

**Skills to use:** `last30days`, `grok-search`

**Output:** 10-20 hook angles + exact audience language in their own words. Store in `[Campaign]_Research.md`.

### Credibility-Led Selling

Source: Naval Ravikant, `Sell the Truth`, nav.al/sell, May 2026.

For trust-sensitive categories, the content should sell by increasing credibility, not pressure. The best proof is often what you refuse to sell.

Principles:
- Tell the truth the buyer needs, even when it disqualifies them.
- Explain simply enough that a smart outsider can follow.
- Move on when the message does not resonate. Do not hammer skeptical viewers into submission.
- Let the product be the answer to a real situation, not the subject of every post.
- Optimize for the people who can see through tactics. If they trust it, everyone else gets easier.

### Trust Ledger Content Pillar

Run 1-2 trust-ledger posts per week: bad-fit posts, limitation posts, tradeoff posts, rejected-example posts, better-alternative posts, postmortem posts. Track separately from conversion ads. Their job is trust density.

### Creative-Fatigue Research Stack

Source: @adamtaylorl X article on beating Meta creative fatigue, May 2026.

Before producing new ads, separate research into three jobs:
1. **Validate demand:** find where spend and repeated creative effort are actually going.
2. **Find native inspiration:** mine organic social for formats that hold attention without looking like ads.
3. **Source customer language:** pull reviews, Reddit threads, comments, support tickets for exact phrasing.

> **Verbalized Sampling — avoid mode collapse:** *"Generate 15 hook angles for [topic]. For each, assign a probability (0–100%) representing how likely a typical AI would produce this exact angle. Mark any below 20% as ⚡ Novel."* Aim for 4–5 ⚡ Novel angles per batch.

## Step 2: Angles & Hooks

Transform research into angles that match audience awareness.

### Match Awareness Level

| Level | Audience State | Angle |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| 1 - Unaware | Don't know problem exists | Lead with problem agitation |
| 2 - Problem Aware | Know problem, not solutions | Validate + introduce solution |
| 3 - Solution Aware | Know solutions, not you | Differentiate your approach |
| 4 - Product Aware | Know you, haven't acted | Address objections, provide proof |
| 5 - Fully Aware | Ready to act | Make offer irresistible |

Cold traffic = Level 1-2. Retargeted = Level 3-4. Email list = Level 4-5.

### Full-Funnel Angle OS

Creative fatigue often means the account has exhausted one awareness level, not that the product needs a new story. Map each major angle across all five awareness levels, then produce at least three format variations per angle. Rule: if every ad mentions the product in the first three seconds, the account is probably overfed on Level 4-5 creative and starving Level 1-2.

### Hook Types

Write all three, then pick the right one for the format:
- **Stated**: what you SAY in copy
- **Visual**: what they SEE in the first frame
- **Audio**: what they HEAR in the first 3 seconds

For Instagram Reels UGC, add a fourth micro-hook: **Audible stim** (diegetic sound in the first 0.5s that breaks scroll autopilot). Source: @Jibran_05, May 2026.

> **Load on-demand:** `hooks` skill has 15+ proven hook formulas.

### Hook Psychology Triggers

| Trigger | What it does | Hook example |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| **Pattern interrupt** | Breaks scroll autopilot | "Stop scrolling if you're ready to level up" |
| **Authority + specificity** | Credibility + concrete action | "I guarantee you'll sleep better if you try this tonight" |
| **Effort reduction** | Lowers perceived friction | "The easiest path to real progress" |
| **Contrast framing** | Replace bad with better in one sentence | "Skip detox teas. Track protein instead." |
| **Identity affirmation** | Viewer feels seen | "To everyone building in silence: this is your nudge" |
| **Curiosity gap** | Withholds the solution | "I finally cracked sustainable productivity" |
| **Loss aversion** | Frames inaction as losing | "Your portfolio is bleeding and you don't know why" |

Pick the trigger first, then write the line. Not the other way around.

### The Wound-First Pattern

Open with a shared fear the audience already carries. Not a product problem, a *life* fear. The product is never the subject. The fear is. The product is the exit. Best for: cold traffic at Level 1-2.

### Social-Failure Tutorial Pattern

Formula: `Here's how to stop failing at [socially visible task], using [simple system anyone can follow].`

Works because of relational stakes, instant audience fit, share mechanics, effort reduction, and system promise. Use for any category where the product helps someone avoid looking clueless in front of another person.

### Format Selection

**15-second silent demo test:** If someone watched a 15s demo with sound off and didn't understand it, no amount of marketing fixes that.

| Format | Why It Works | Best For |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Notes App | Looks like personal content | Problem-solution, starter packs |
| Text-Over-Video | Story through text sequence | Transformations, confessions |
| Reddit/Tweet Screenshot | Discovery energy | Hot takes, personal stories |
| Meme | Culturally native | Contrasts, humor |
| Documentary/Talking Head | High trust, like journalism | Skeptical audiences, finance |
| Hands-Doing-Something | Tactile task anchors attention | Tutorials, productivity |
| Tier List | Gamified ranking; comments argue | Tool reviews, comparisons |
| Three-Screen App Demo | Input → processing → result | Consumer apps, AI utilities |
| Investigation Arc | Find contradiction, investigate, reveal | Anti-ad UGC, finance, health |
| Before/After [Product] | Two-word text overlay + emotional transformation | AI tools, creative apps, fitness |

### Anti-Ad Execution

Source: @adamtaylorl X article on beating Meta creative fatigue, May 2026.

Execution rules:
- Loose creator briefs: hook, proof points, claims to avoid, customer phrases. Don't script every line.
- Build around human conflict or discovery, not product explanation.
- Product appears as the thing discovered, not the thing announced.

**The 80/20 emotion-to-product ratio:** Best anti-ads spend ~80% of runtime on emotion, ~20% on product proof (a quick app UI flash). If the feature demo exceeds 30%, it's an ad again. (Source: Suno "Before/After" ad, May 2026)

**No-CTA as positioning:** Some of the strongest ads end on emotion, not a sales pitch. Works when content is shared as entertainment, the product name is in the hook text, and a CTA would cheapen the payoff. Reserve for brand-building, not direct response.

### Three-Screen App Demo

Design the product so an influencer can explain it in three screens: (1) input, (2) magical processing, (3) result worth showing. If those aren't obvious, creators explain too much. (Source: @JosephKChoi / Cal AI, May 2026)

### Viral Feature Test

Ask whether the app has a viral feature: a single visual moment that satisfies a core human desire and can be shown in one frame. Formula: `core human desire + one-frame reveal = ad-native product loop`. If the reveal can't be understood muted in 1-2 seconds, fix the product surface before buying ads. (Source: @athcanft, Apr 2026)

## Step 2b: Pre-Creator Validation

**Recreate before you recruit.** Prove a format works on your own account before committing creator budget.

1. Warm up the account. See `references/tiktok-warmup.md`.
2. Save viral videos from <1k follower accounts (pre-algo signals).
3. Analyze why they worked: hook structure, comment sentiment, format, pacing.
4. Recreate the format yourself.
5. See early traction before sourcing paid creators.
6. Pre-score finished cuts when a predictor tool is available. (Source: @johnvirality on Higgsfield `brain_activity`, May 2026)

### Obsession Filter

Do not build content pillars around topics the creator cannot genuinely sustain. Use before committing: Can the creator talk about this without notes for 10 minutes? Do they naturally collect examples?

### Daniel / Cal AI / Sway Playbook

## Step 2c: Video Series Planning

When the output is an AI character video series (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) rather than a single post.

1. Load character config from `$CHARACTERS_DIR/<slug>/config.json`
2. Define the series: topic cluster + episode count + arc
3. Generate episode ideas (10-20) using Verbalized Sampling
4. Score each idea on: hook strength, shareability, niche relevance, evergreen vs timely, production simplicity

Script format: `HOOK (0-3s) → SETUP (3-10s) → BODY (10-50s, 3 points max) → CTA (last 3s)`. **No-intro rule:** Never use the first 3 seconds to introduce yourself. Jump directly to the hook.

## Step 2e: Result-First Product Proof Loop (WayShot Model)

Lead with the aspirational result, then reveal the ordinary input. This is after/before, not before/after. Beautiful outcome first, flat source second, app mention last. Bookmarks are the leading signal for save-engine formats.

> **Load on-demand:** `references/result-first-proof-loop.md` for the WayShot case study (150M+ views, $100K MRR), the three-format sequence, and Bloom adaptations.

## Step 2f: Repeatable Daily Format (The Oasis Model)

One format, repeated daily, with only the subject changing. The Oasis model ($23K MRR, 232 Reels, identical format) and the Suno AI serialized format (parts 20-65, hundreds of thousands of views each) prove this works. The generalized pattern: `[raw material with built-in curiosity] + [AI/tool transformation] + [serial numbering] = compounding series`.

> **Load on-demand:** `references/repeatable-daily-format.md` for both case studies, the Serialized AI Transformation Pattern table, Bloom format candidates, part numbering strategy, and referral-as-gate mechanics.

## Step 2g: Conversion Bridge (Views → Install → Payment)

Views without installs = entertainment. Match CTA patterns to format and awareness level. Cold traffic should never see "download now." Track the profile-visit funnel weekly (view → profile visit → link click → install). Use comment-as-distribution mechanics to turn engagement into algorithm fuel.

## Step 4: Distribute

| Platform | Tool |
|---------|------|
| LinkedIn, X, Threads | `typefully` skill |
| TikTok | ReelFarm / slideshow tools |

**Queue minimums:**

| Platform | Min Queue | Cadence |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| LinkedIn | 7 posts | 1/day |
| X | 14 posts | 2/day |
| TikTok | 14-21 posts | 2-3/day |
| Threads | 7 posts | 1/day |

Batch at least 7 days per session. Never schedule one post at a time. If queue drops below 3 days, refill immediately.

**TikTok at scale:** Batch-create 10-30 pieces per session. Source visuals from Pinterest/Freepik, generate hooks via ChatGPT, assemble in Canva, schedule via Postiz (open-source). (Source: @alexcooldev, May 2026)

### Geo-Targeting (Instagram / Reels)

Regional markets have lower CPMs, less competition, and engaged audiences. Use native location tags for specific business districts, not just city names. Adapt hooks to match dominant industry in target city.

## Step 5: Analyze & Optimize

### Decision Rules (Post Level)

| Views | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 50K+ | DOUBLE DOWN — make 3 variations immediately |
| 10K-50K | Good — keep in rotation, test small tweaks |
| 1K-10K | Okay — try 1 variation before dropping |
| <1K (twice) | DROP — radically different approach needed |

### Two-Axis Diagnostic

| Views | Conversions | Diagnosis | Fix |
|-------|-------------|-----------|-----|
| High | High | Scale it | Make variations, increase frequency |
| High | Low | CTA problem | Hook works, downstream is broken |
| Low | High | Hook problem | Content converts, needs more reach |
| Low | Low | Full reset | Try radically different approach |

### Angle Leaderboard and Creative Compounding

When a creative works, log: source, format, concept, angle, hook, persona, awareness level, visual structure, CTA, spend, views, CTR, CVR, CPA, revenue. Compound winners by recombining proven parts: swap a winning hook onto a previous winning body, turn a video into a static, scale an angle into a new format. (Source: @adamtaylorl, May 2026)

### Walk-Away Positioning

Some offers get more credible when they show willingness to walk away. Publish honest bad-fit criteria. Explain dealbreakers before benefits. Avoid desperation CTAs. (Source: Naval Ravikant, `Sell the Truth`, May 2026)

### Monthly Pillar Review (Run on the 1st)

| Status | Criteria | Action |
|--------|---------|--------|
| SCALE | High views + high conversions | Increase frequency |
| KEEP | Decent and stable | Hold cadence |
| ELEVATE | Underperforming but sound concept | Change one lever |
| ROTATE OUT | 2+ months underperforming after elevation | Move to bench |
