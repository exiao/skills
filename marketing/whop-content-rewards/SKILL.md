# Whop Content Rewards

Launch and manage pay-per-view UGC campaigns on Whop's Content Rewards platform.

## When to Use

- Launch a new UGC campaign
- Add budget to an existing campaign
- Review submissions and creator quality
- Improve campaign briefs, hooks, and payout rules
- Diagnose why a Content Rewards campaign is not getting good submissions

## Core Model

Whop Content Rewards lets brands pay creators for qualified posts, usually on TikTok, Reels, or Shorts. The campaign brief defines the offer, required talking points, disallowed claims, payout terms, and approval criteria.

The goal is not polished influencer content. The best submissions feel native to the feed: lo-fi, specific, and creator-led.

## Campaign Setup Checklist

### 1. Define the offer

Capture:
- Product name: `$PRODUCT_NAME`
- Product category: `$PRODUCT_CATEGORY`
- One-line promise: `$PRODUCT_PROMISE`
- Primary audience: `$TARGET_AUDIENCE`
- Landing page or app link: `$DESTINATION_URL`
- Required disclosure language, if any

Keep the promise specific. Bad: "the best app for everything." Better: "helps busy retail investors understand why their stocks are moving."

### 2. Set payout mechanics

Decide:
- Qualified platforms: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
- Minimum view threshold
- Payout per qualified view or per approved post
- Max payout per creator
- Total campaign budget
- Required post duration
- Whether creators can post multiple videos

Prefer a small initial test budget, then add budget after you see creator quality and CPM equivalents.

### 3. Write the creator brief

Include:
- What the product does
- Who it is for
- What creators should show or say
- What claims are banned
- What native formats work
- Examples of strong hooks
- Submission requirements

The brief should be tight enough to prevent bad claims, but loose enough that creators can make native content.

## Generic Creator Brief Template

### Campaign name

`$PRODUCT_NAME` UGC Campaign

### Product description

`$PRODUCT_NAME` helps `$TARGET_AUDIENCE` get `$DREAM_OUTCOME` without `$PAINFUL_ALTERNATIVE`.

### Target audience

Describe the buyer in plain language:
- Who they are
- What they are trying to do
- What frustrates them today
- What language they already use

Example:
"Busy people who want to understand their money but hate spreadsheets, jargon, and bouncing between five apps."

### Creative direction

Make short, native-feeling videos. Do not hard sell. The product should appear as part of the story, not as a pitch deck.

Good formats:
- "I tried X so you do not have to"
- "Things I wish I knew before..."
- POV problem moment
- Screen recording with honest narration
- Before and after workflow
- Reaction to a common mistake
- "Nobody told me..."
- Myth vs reality

### Required talking points

- `$CORE_BENEFIT_1`
- `$CORE_BENEFIT_2`
- `$CORE_BENEFIT_3`

### Optional angles

- Frustration with current workflow
- Anxiety about missing something
- Comparison to the old way
- Small moment of relief
- Discovery story
- Social proof, if approved

### Do not say

- Unsupported performance claims
- Guaranteed outcomes
- Medical, financial, legal, or income guarantees unless explicitly approved
- Fake testimonials
- Claims that imply official endorsement by a platform or third party

### Example hooks

Replace these with category-specific hooks:
- "I did not realize how much time I was wasting on this until I tried..."
- "If you use [category], stop doing this manually."
- "This is the app I wish I had before..."
- "I tested [product] for a week. Here is what surprised me."
- "POV: you finally found a tool that does this in one place."

## Creator Quality Rules

Approve creators who:
- Sound like real users, not ad readers
- Understand the audience pain
- Can show the product naturally
- Have recent posts with normal engagement
- Do not rely only on generic AI voiceover

Reject creators who:
- Make every post look like a templated ad
- Use fake urgency or scammy captions
- Promise impossible outcomes
- Misrepresent the product
- Reuse the same script across unrelated products

## Submission Review Checklist

For each submission, check:
1. Does the video satisfy platform and campaign requirements?
2. Is the claim compliant?
3. Does the hook make sense in the first 2 seconds?
4. Does the product appear naturally?
5. Would the target audience believe this came from a real user?
6. Is the CTA clear but not overbearing?
7. Is the creator worth inviting for more posts?

## Optimization Loop

Every few days:
1. Export submissions and performance.
2. Group by format: screen recording, talking head, meme, listicle, story, comparison.
3. Identify winners by views, saves, comments, CTR, and qualified cost.
4. Add budget to formats that work.
5. Tighten the brief around winning hooks.
6. Remove confusing or risky instructions.
7. Add examples of approved submissions.

## Common Mistakes

- Overwriting the creator's voice with brand copy
- Making product demo mandatory for the full video
- Asking for too many talking points
- Hiding the actual use case behind vague benefit language
- Approving claims that paid ads could not legally run
- Judging only views instead of creator quality and audience fit

## Output Format

When asked to create or improve a campaign, return:

1. Campaign objective
2. Target audience
3. Creator brief
4. Required talking points
5. Banned claims
6. Example hooks
7. Submission approval checklist
8. Budget and payout recommendation
9. Optimization plan
