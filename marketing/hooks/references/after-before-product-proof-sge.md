# After-Before Product Proof (SGE / WayShot)

Source: Social Growth Engineers WayShot case study, May 2026: `https://www.socialgrowthengineers.com/how-a-photo-editor-hit-6-in-the-u-s-with-150m-views`

## Case study numbers

WayShot, an aesthetic photo editor, reportedly reached:
- 150M+ views across 8 accounts
- $100K MRR
- 500K+ downloads in one month
- #6 in the U.S. App Store
- One organic identity format at ~48.6M views
- One product slideshow at ~7.5M views
- Newer variation with 4 videos over 2M views in April 2026, strongest at ~7M views and 230K bookmarks

## Core idea

For visual products, the strongest proof often comes from reversing the usual demo order:

1. Show the aspirational result first.
2. Reveal the ordinary original/input second.
3. Mention the app/product last, usually in the caption or account identity.

This is not before/after. It is after/before. The result creates desire; the reveal creates shock; the product gets credited without feeling like an ad.

## Three-system sequence

WayShot did not start with direct response. It stacked formats by funnel job:

1. **Audience builder:** `That friend who thinks everything is aesthetic:`
   - Native, relatable, barely promotional.
   - Product signal mostly through account name.
   - Job: reach, comments, shares, follower growth.
2. **Product proof:** `she edits her photos too much` / `she uses too many filters`
   - Two-slide faceless slideshow: edited result first, original second.
   - Caption credits the app.
   - Job: make the transformation obvious without explaining it.
3. **Save-worthy flex:** `What camera did you use?` → `Oh it's my phone`
   - Makes the result feel aspirational but accessible.
   - Job: bookmarks/saves because viewers want to recreate it.

## Winning hooks

### 1. Relatable identity hook

`That friend who thinks everything is [aesthetic / cinematic / optimized / investable]`

Use for top-of-funnel audience growth. The content should be native and shareable even if nobody knows the product exists. Product signal can be only the account name.

### 2. Social critique hook

`she [edits / optimizes / tracks / analyzes] too much`

Structure:
- Slide 1: impressive final result
- Slide 2: plain original
- Reply/reveal: `damn you're right`
- Caption: `edited by [app]` or equivalent

Use when the audience already recognizes the behavior and can tease themselves or a friend about it.

### 3. Tool confusion hook

`What [camera / tool / app / setup] did you use?`

Reveal:
`Oh it's my [phone / notes app / broker screen / data export]`

Use when the result looks like it required expensive gear or expertise, but your product makes it accessible.

## Why it works

- It feels native because the hook is a social observation, not a product claim.
- It creates proof without instruction. The contrast does the selling.
- It drives bookmarks when the result is aspirational and replicable.
- It creates comments because viewers ask how it was made or tag the person who behaves that way.

## Adaptation checklist

- Is the first frame/result genuinely attractive enough to stop the scroll?
- Is the original/input ordinary enough to create contrast?
- Can the product be credited subtly through caption, account name, watermark, or final card?
- Would the post still be shareable if the viewer ignored the product?
- Is there a save-worthy path for the viewer to recreate the outcome?
- Are you using the right funnel job? Audience builder first, product proof second, save-worthy flex third.
- Are saves/bookmarks high? For this format, bookmarks can matter more than comments because the viewer wants the result later.
- Did you resist explaining the product? The visual contrast should do the work.

## Bloom examples

- `That friend who thinks every stock is a thesis:` Show a polished Bloom card first, then reveal the chaotic Reddit thread or headline soup it came from.
- `he analyzes stocks too much` → polished AI thesis card → raw brokerage chart → `damn you're right`.
- `What analyst did you use?` → `Oh it's my phone.` Show Bloom insight card, then phone screen.
