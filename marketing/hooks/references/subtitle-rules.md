# Subtitle Rules

The subtitle's job is to give just enough detail to confirm the click is worth it, while deepening the curiosity:
- Include a specific number, contrast, or concrete upgrade not in the title
- Foreshadow the payoff without revealing every detail
- Should feel like the natural "second sentence" after the title
- On paywalls, clarity beats cleverness: the subtitle should answer "what do I get if I pay?" or "what changes from free?"

**Good:**
- Title: "I Deleted Half My AI's Prompt. It Got Smarter."
- Subtitle: "Then I tested it 363 times and everything broke. But the failures told me exactly what to add back."
- Paywall title: "Your thinking partner for investing"
- Paywall subtitle: "Upgrade from quick answers to full stock research"

**Bad:**
- Subtitle: "A guide to optimizing AI prompts with automated testing" (boring, no tension)
- Paywall subtitle: "Go deeper before you invest" (too vague alone; it does not explain the paid/free delta)
