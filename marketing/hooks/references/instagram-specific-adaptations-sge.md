# Instagram-Specific Adaptations (SGE)

- **Reels can outperform TikTok** for certain verticals (language learning, faith apps)
- Convert TikTok slideshows to IG by making each slide 1-2 second video frames
- **Carousel posts** work differently than Reels. Use for listicles, "your style is," aesthetic content
- Professor/educational content often does better on IG Reels than TikTok
- Cross-post the same hook in both formats but track performance separately

## Reel hook stack: text first, speech second

Source: Adrian Per, `@omgadrian`, Instagram Reel `DG-5t5lJxOO`, Mar 2025.

For Reels, assume the first 1-3 seconds are watched muted. The text overlay should carry the full hook before the voice matters.

Strong stack:
1. **Outcome:** `HOW TO TAKE BETTER PICS`
2. **Audience:** `OF YOUR GF (OR BF)`
3. **Bonus value:** `+ HOW TO POSE`
4. **Framework tease:** `3 common categories`

Reusable shell:
`HOW TO [get desired outcome] FOR [specific person/situation] + [unexpected adjacent benefit]`

Bloom examples:
- `HOW TO RESEARCH STOCKS WITHOUT PANICKING + WHAT TO IGNORE`
- `HOW TO CHECK YOUR PORTFOLIO LIKE AN ANALYST + WHAT TO FIX FIRST`
- `HOW TO READ EARNINGS WITHOUT FINANCE BRAINROT + THE 3 SIGNALS THAT MATTER`

Why it works: the viewer instantly knows the outcome, who it is for, and that there is a system coming. The extra benefit makes the video feel denser than a normal tutorial.

Success diagnosis for Adrian's Reel:
- It names a universal relationship pain, not a generic skill gap. "Your partner hates your photos" has stakes; "photo tips" does not.
- It is useful to both sides of the relationship: the person taking the photo and the person posing.
- It is easy to share as a playful accusation. A viewer can send it to a partner without writing anything.
- It proves credibility visually. The aesthetic location, attractive subject, confident delivery, and actual pose demos make the lesson self-evident.
- It lowers effort with normal tools. "Just your phone" beats camera-nerd advice.

## Relationship-gap tutorial hook

Source: Adrian Per, `@omgadrian`, Instagram Reel `DG-5t5lJxOO`, Mar 2025.

A tutorial gets stronger when it names a social failure, not just a skill gap. Adrian does not open with "photo composition tips." He opens with the emotional situation: partners hating the photos someone takes of them.

Formula:
`I know [person/group] keeps messing up [socially visible task]. Here's how to fix it with [simple constraint].`

Rules:
- Name the person who feels judged: boyfriend, girlfriend, founder, retail investor, PM, analyst.
- Name the social consequence: looks clueless, gets roasted, disappoints someone, misses the obvious thing.
- Then lower the effort: with just your phone, in 3 categories, in 5 minutes, without a spreadsheet.

Bloom examples:
- `I know your portfolio makes you feel like you guessed. Here's how to review it in 3 passes.`
- `Most people read earnings like homework. Here's the 3-signal version.`
- `If your friend asked why you own that stock, could you answer? Here's the 5-minute check.`

## Framework reveal pacing

Adrian's reel front-loads the promise, then slows down after commitment:

`big text hook -> creator context -> framework list -> demonstration -> result/examples`

Use this for educational Reels. Do not start with the framework. Earn the framework by first naming the painful situation or desired outcome.
