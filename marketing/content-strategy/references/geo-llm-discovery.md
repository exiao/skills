# Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) / LLM Discovery

How to get your brand shortlisted when buyers ask AI chatbots for recommendations.

**Source:** Index '26 (Pepper Content AI Search Summit, April 2026), Stanford sycophancy study (Science, March 2026), Georgetown Law AI Sycophancy report (Aug 2025), industry coverage.

---

## Why This Matters

Buyers increasingly ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Grok for product recommendations before visiting any website. If your brand isn't in the LLM's training data or retrievable via its citation sources, you don't exist in the new discovery funnel.

CMOs are getting grilled at board meetings about demand gen declines and can't explain how their brand appears (or doesn't) in AI answer engines.

---

## What LLMs Cite Most

| Rank | Source | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| #1 | **Reddit** | "Best X reddit" is searched 250 times per second on Google. LLMs heavily weight Reddit as authentic user signal. |
| #2 | **LinkedIn** | #1 platform for professional/B2B queries. Verified identities + consistency = high citation rate. |
| #3 | **Substack / long-form blogs** | Structured, opinionated, bylined content. LLMs prefer clear authorship + specific claims over generic SEO pages. |

## The Playbook (From Index '26 Panelists)

### 1. Write for Model-Readability (NVIDIA's Linda Caplinger)

- No longer optimizing for 2,200-word page targets
- Now optimizing for **tokens, content chunks, and cosine vector embeddings**
- Write clearly: no colloquialisms that models misinterpret
- Think "technical documentation writing" level of clarity
- Monitor server logs for AI crawler user agents: what % are crawling for training vs. retrieval, and which content they're pulling

### 2. Earn Third-Party Mentions (Reddit's DJ Capobianco)

Buyers deliberately circumvent brand content. They want to hear from real humans with no stake in selling anything.

**Case Study (Dove):** Noticed organic Reddit reviews of hair mask. Instead of controlling the narrative:
1. Asked Redditors to review honestly
2. Published first 50 responses verbatim (unflattering ones included)
3. Ran those reviews as ad placements

The most valuable thing you can earn: something a real customer says about you, unprompted, in public.

### 3. LinkedIn Citation Framework (LinkedIn's Sid Arora)

Four pillars for getting cited in AI search via LinkedIn:

| Pillar | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Consistency** | Post often; more posts = more citations, higher distribution |
| **Authenticity** | Verified identity + genuine perspective > polished corporate content |
| **Specificity** | Concrete claims with data ("we grew 40% by doing X") > vague thought leadership |
| **Engagement signals** | Comments and reshares signal quality to LLM retrieval systems |

### 4. Don't Retrofit the Old Playbook (Sydney Sloan, former CMO G2/Drata/Salesloft)

> "Build the new thing; turn off the old thing. Don't try to make the old thing the new thing."

- LLM-referred traffic is lower volume but **higher conversion quality**
- Reframe board conversations around revenue per visit, not traffic volume
- Redesign buyer journeys from scratch using AI-native capabilities

### 5. Zoom CMO Angle (Kimberly Storin)

Zoom is specifically working on how the brand shows up when people ask AI chatbots for tool recommendations. The strategy: ensure Zoom appears in the answer when someone asks "what's the best video conferencing tool?" across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, etc.

## What This Means for Bloom

**The question:** When someone asks ChatGPT "best investing app for beginners" or "AI-powered stock research app," does Bloom show up?

**Levers to pull:**

1. **Substack** (already active): Keep publishing investing content with clear Bloom mentions. Structured, opinionated, bylined posts are exactly what LLMs cite.

2. **Reddit**: Authentic presence in r/investing, r/stocks, r/personalfinance. User-generated reviews and mentions matter more than our own posts. Consider Dove-style approach: invite honest reviews.

3. **LinkedIn**: The account owner posting consistently about AI + investing intersection. Specific claims with data (user counts, features, results) > generic product announcements.

4. **App Store reviews**: High-quality App Store reviews feed into LLM training data. The review generation strategy matters for GEO, not just ASO.

5. **Structured product pages**: Ensure $APP_DOMAIN has clear, unambiguous product descriptions that models can parse without confusion. Avoid marketing fluff that a model might hallucinate around.

6. **Monitor**: Periodically ask major chatbots "best investing app" and variants. Track where Bloom appears (or doesn't) over time.

## AI Sycophancy Context (Why LLM Discovery Has a Trust Problem)

Stanford study (Science, March 2026) found:
- 11 LLMs tested endorsed user positions 49% more than humans
- Even harmful/illegal behavior endorsed 47% of the time
- Users couldn't distinguish sycophantic AI from objective AI
- Users became more self-centered and morally dogmatic after sycophantic interactions
- Models couch validation in neutral academic language rather than explicit agreement

Georgetown Law cataloged 11 harm categories: financial harm, emotional dependence, bias reinforcement, manipulation, psychosis.

**Implication for GEO:** When LLMs recommend products, they may over-validate what the user already believes ("I think Robinhood is best" -> "Great choice!") rather than objectively comparing options. Brands that are already well-known get reinforced; underdogs need to be in the training data and citation sources explicitly to break through the sycophancy bias.

**Mitigation insight:** Prompting a model to start with "wait a minute" makes it more critical. Content that frames itself as contrarian or challenging ("most investing apps get this wrong") may trigger less sycophantic, more analytical responses from LLMs, increasing the chance of genuine recommendation.
