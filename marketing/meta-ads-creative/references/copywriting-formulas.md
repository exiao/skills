# Copywriting Formulas for Meta Ads

**Purpose:** Battle-tested copy structures for writing Meta ad primary text, headlines, and descriptions.

---

## The Meta Ads Value Formula

Before any copywriting, remember:

**Value = Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood / Time Delay x Effort**

Your copy must communicate:
- **Dream Outcome:** What transformation do they want?
- **Perceived Likelihood:** Will this work for THEM?
- **Time Delay:** How long until results?
- **Effort Required:** How hard is this?

**Maximize the top (outcome x likelihood), minimize the bottom (time x effort).**

## PRIMARY TEXT FORMULAS

### PAS (Problem - Agitate - Solution)

**Structure:**
```
[PROBLEM - State the pain point clearly]

[AGITATE - Make the problem feel more urgent/painful]

[SOLUTION - Introduce your solution as the answer]

[CTA - What to do next]
```

**Example:**
```
Managing your money sounds simple until you see how many tabs it takes.

Brokerage, bank app, spreadsheets, watchlists, newsletters...
it adds up fast. And suddenly "staying informed" feels like another job.

What if you could get the important context in one place,
without rebuilding your workflow?

[Product] gives busy investors a faster way to understand what matters.
Try it in 5 minutes.
```

**Best For:**
- Problem-aware audiences
- High pain points
- Clear before/after

---

### AIDA (Attention - Interest - Desire - Action)

**Structure:**
```
[ATTENTION - Hook that stops the scroll]

[INTEREST - Build curiosity with benefits/features]

[DESIRE - Create wanting with outcomes/proof]

[ACTION - Clear CTA]
```

**Example:**
```
I went from checking five investing apps every morning to one.

Same portfolio. Same goals. Better workflow.

Turns out I was wasting energy collecting context
instead of making decisions. I tried [Product].

Now I can see the news, risks, and next questions faster.
Less tab chaos, more clarity.

[Product] takes 5 minutes to try.
```

**Best For:**
- Cold audiences
- Transformation stories
- Building progressive interest

---

### Before-After-Bridge

**Structure:**
```
[BEFORE - Current painful state]

[AFTER - Desired outcome achieved]

[BRIDGE - How to get from Before to After (your offer)]
```

**Example:**
```
BEFORE: Ten tabs open. Three conflicting opinions.
A decision you keep postponing.

AFTER: The key facts, risks, and next questions are clear.
You know what to look into next.

THE BRIDGE: [Product] turns scattered research into a simple investing workflow.

Try it in 5 minutes.
```

**Best For:**
- Transformation stories
- Testimonial-based ads
- Emotional impact

---

### Testimonial Formula

**Structure:**
```
"[Direct quote from customer]"

[Context - who they are, what they faced]

[Outcome - specific result]

[Bridge to offer]

[CTA]
```

**Example:**
```
"I finally understand why my stocks are moving without reading five different sites."

This user was tired of bouncing between news, charts, and analyst notes
just to understand what changed.

With [Product], the important context lives in one workflow.
Less hunting, more useful questions.

Join thousands of users who want clearer investing research.
```

**Best For:**
- Warm audiences
- Building trust
- Specific outcomes

### Story Formula

**Structure:**
```
[HOOK - Intriguing opening]

[SETUP - Quick context]

[CONFLICT - The problem faced]

[TURNING POINT - What changed]

[RESOLUTION - The outcome]

[LESSON/CTA - What this means for reader]
```

**Example:**
```
I thought I was bad at keeping up with the market.

Every week, another earnings report, rate headline, or stock move.
I kept saving articles and never reading them.

Then I tried [Product]. It gave me the context first,
so I knew what deserved more attention.

Within a week, my research felt less scattered.

The problem was never curiosity.
It was the workflow.

Try [Product] in 5 minutes.
```

**Best For:**
- Emotional connection
- Longer form ads
- Parent identification

---

### List/Listicle Formula

**Structure:**
```
[HOOK - Number + promise]

[LIST - 3-7 items with brief explanations]

[SUMMARY - What this means]

[CTA]
```

**Example:**
```
5 things I wish I knew before building an investing routine:

1. You do not need every headline
2. One clear workflow beats five dashboards
3. Risks matter as much as upside
4. Good questions beat hot takes
5. Consistency matters more than checking constantly

[Product] helped me turn scattered research into a repeatable habit.

Join thousands of users. Try it in 5 minutes.
```

**Best For:**
- Educational content
- Value-packed ads
- Scannable format

---

### "What If" Formula

**Structure:**
```
[WHAT IF - Pose the possibility]

[EXPAND - Build the vision]

[REALITY - Show it's real/achievable]

[HOW - Your solution]

[CTA]
```

**Example:**
```
What if staying on top of your stocks did not require five apps?

What if you could see the news, risks, and research prompts
for your watchlist in one place...

Without spending your morning tab-hopping?

That is [Product]. A faster research workflow for busy investors.

Thousands of users already joined. Try it in 5 minutes.
```

**Best For:**
- Possibility thinking
- Budget-conscious audiences
- Overcoming objections

---

### "Confession" Formula

**Structure:**
```
[CONFESSION - Admit something vulnerable]

[CONTEXT - Why you felt that way]

[DISCOVERY - What you learned/found]

[OUTCOME - How it changed things]

[INVITATION - Offer to reader]
```

**Example:**
```
I'll be honest: I used to avoid looking at my portfolio.

Was I missing something? Was the move already over?
Was I just reacting to headlines?

Then I found [Product]. News, context, risks, and follow-up questions in one place.

I went from scattered checking to a simple research habit.

If your investing workflow feels messy, try it in 5 minutes.
```

**Best For:**
- Building trust
- Addressing objections
- Relatable content

## HOOK FORMULAS

### Curiosity Gap Hooks

```
"Nobody talks about [hidden thing]..."
"The thing no one tells you about [topic]..."
"I discovered something about [topic] that changed everything..."
"There's a reason [surprising fact]..."
```

### Transformation Hooks

```
"My [person] went from [before] to [after]..."
"[Before state] to [after state] in [time]..."
"One year ago [before]. Today [after]..."
"The difference between [failure] and [success]..."
```

### Authority Hooks

```
"After [time/experience] in [field], here's what I learned..."
"[Number] users discovered this..."
"The secret [experts] won't tell you..."
```

### Problem Callout Hooks

```
"If your workflow [problem sign], read this..."
"Stop [common mistake]..."
"[Problem]? There's another way..."
"The problem isn't your workflow. It's [real problem]..."
```

### Social Proof Hooks

```
"5,000+ users discovered..."
"This one thing changed everything for us..."
"Why [number] users are doing [action]..."
"[Result] - here's how..."
```

### POV/Future Hooks

```
"POV: It's [future date] and [desired outcome]..."
"Imagine [desired scenario]..."
"Picture this: [outcome visualization]..."
```

### Permission Hooks

```
"This is your sign to [action]..."
"You're allowed to [permission]..."
"What if [possibility]..."
```

## HEADLINE FORMULAS

Headlines appear below the media. Keep them short (5-8 words max).

### Benefit-Focused

```
"[Outcome] Made Possible"
"[Desired State] Without [Obstacle]"
"Finally, [Solution]"
```

**Examples:**
- "Research Made Simpler"
- "Better Context Without the Chaos"
- "Finally, Research That Works"

### Question Headlines

```
"What If [Possibility]?"
"Ready for [Outcome]?"
"Struggling with [Problem]?"
```

**Examples:**
- "What If Research Was Clear?"
- "Ready for a Cleaner Workflow?"
- "Struggling with Too Many Tabs?"

### Statement Headlines

```
"[Counterintuitive Truth]"
"[Bold Claim]"
"[Simple Promise]"
```

**Examples:**
- "Maybe the Problem Is the Workflow"
- "Premium Context, Less Chaos"
- "Research That Actually Fits"

### Social Proof Headlines

```
"Join [Number]+ [Audience]"
"Why [Number] Users Switched"
"[Number] Users Discovered This"
```

**Examples:**
- "Join Thousands of Users"
- "Why Users Switched"
- "The Workflow Users Love"

## AWARENESS LEVEL COPY MATCHING

### Level 1: Unaware
*Don't know they have a problem*

**Approach:** Lead with problem/pain agitation
**Hook Style:** Pattern interrupt, surprising fact
**Copy Length:** Longer, more education needed

**Example Hook:**
"Your workflow spends 7 hours a day in a system designed in 1892..."

### Level 2: Problem Aware
*Know the problem, don't know solutions exist*

**Approach:** Validate problem, introduce solution category
**Hook Style:** "If you're struggling with..."
**Copy Length:** Medium, balance education and solution

**Example Hook:**
"Frustrated with traditional school? There's another way..."

### Level 3: Solution Aware
*Know solutions exist, don't know your product*

**Approach:** Differentiate your approach
**Hook Style:** Comparison, unique mechanism
**Copy Length:** Medium, focus on differentiators

**Example Hook:**
"Unlike other tools, [Product] gives you..."

### Level 4: Product Aware
*Know your product, haven't acted*

**Approach:** Address objections, provide proof
**Hook Style:** Testimonials, specific results
**Copy Length:** Shorter, more direct

**Example Hook:**
"See why thousands of users chose [Product]..."

### Level 5: Fully Aware
*Know and want your product*

**Approach:** Make offer irresistible
**Hook Style:** Direct CTA, urgency
**Copy Length:** Short, action-focused

**Example Hook:**
"Apply now - Fall 2026 spots filling fast"

## OBJECTION HANDLING IN COPY

### Common Objections & Copy Solutions

**"Am I qualified?"**
```
"You don't need technical expertise. You need workflow
(included), support (included), and community (included)."
```

**"Can we afford it?"**
```
"[Product] keeps pricing simple. The core workflow is included,
so you are not forced into a maze of add-ons."
```

**"Will they fall behind?"**
```
"My son went from being behind in math to a a week ahead.
When learning fits the workflow, they don't fall behind - they accelerate."
```

**"What about support?"**
```
"With templates, examples, integrations, and support,
our workflows have MORE meaningful social time, not less."
```

**"I don't have time."**
```
"Most users finish core learning in 3 focused hours.
No more homework battles. No more exhausted evenings."
```

## CTA FORMULAS

### Low Commitment
```
"Learn more"
"See how it works"
"Discover the difference"
```

### Medium Commitment
```
"Start your journey"
"See if you qualify"
"Get the details"
```

### High Commitment
```
"Apply now"
"Join today"
"Enroll now"
```

**Match CTA to Audience Temperature:**
- Cold → Low commitment
- Warm → Medium commitment
- Hot → High commitment

## COPY LENGTH GUIDELINES

### Primary Text

**Short (Under 125 characters):**
- Shows fully in feed
- Best for hot audiences
- Direct, punchy messaging

**Medium (125-500 characters):**
- First 125 visible, rest behind "See More"
- Hook must be strong
- Good for warm audiences

**Long (500-2000 characters):**
- Story-based content
- Educational value
- Cold audiences needing more info

### Headlines
- 5-8 words maximum
- Clear benefit or outcome
- Works standalone

### Descriptions
- 1 short sentence
- Urgency, clarity, or proof
- Supports but doesn't repeat headline
