# UX Research Question Bank

Use these during setup phase and to seed the interview. Pick based on where the conversation needs to go. All questions are designed to be non-leading and open-ended.

---

## Opening / Warm-Up
Get them talking naturally before going deep.

- Tell me about your typical day.
- Walk me through what yesterday looked like for you.
- What's the first thing you do when you [relevant activity]?
- How long have you been dealing with [problem area]?

---

## Goals & Motivations (Jobs-to-be-Done)
Understand what progress they're trying to make.

- What are you ultimately trying to accomplish here?
- What does success look like for you in [domain]?
- Why does that matter to you right now?
- If this were working perfectly, what would be different about your life?

---

## Problem & Friction
Uncover where things break down.

- Walk me through the last time you tried to do [task]. What happened?
- Where do you get stuck most often?
- What's the most frustrating part of [activity]?
- How do you currently handle [problem]? What's broken about that?
- What do you wish you could do that you can't right now?

---

## Current Solutions & Workarounds
Understand the alternatives they've tried.

- What do you use today to handle this?
- How did you find that solution?
- What made you try it? What made you stop (or keep going)?
- What would need to be true for you to switch to something new?

---

## Emotional / Social Layer
Surface the non-functional needs.

- How does it make you feel when [problem] happens?
- Is this something you talk about with others?
- What would it mean to you if this were solved?
- Do you ever feel judged or compared to others in this area?

---

## Validation (Problem / Promise / Product Fit)
Confirm or challenge your 4 P assumptions.

- How often does this come up for you — daily, weekly?
- On a scale of urgency, where would you put this compared to other things on your plate?
- If someone offered you [promise], what would be your first reaction?
- What would make you trust that [promise] was real?
- What's the first thing you'd want to try if you had [product]?
- What would make you pay for something like this?

---

## Wrap-Up
Close out and extract meta-insight.

- Is there anything about this topic I haven't asked that you think matters?
- What's the one thing you'd want whoever builds this to know?
- Who else do you know who deals with this?

---

## Auto-Interview Seed Questions (fallbacks when conversation hasn't started)

Used when no suggested questions exist yet and the researcher wants to kick things off:

1. Tell me about your typical day.
2. Walk me through the last time you tried to solve [problem].
3. What's your biggest frustration with how you handle [problem] today?
4. What does success look like for you in [domain]?
5. What have you already tried? What happened?
6. What would make you trust a new solution in this space?
